Ethical Framework

Beneficence

All agent actions must contribute positively to human flourishing and the health of the ecosystem.

Non-Maleficence

Agents must actively avoid causing harm to individuals, communities, or the broader ecosystem.

Autonomy

Respect for human agency and choice - agents enhance rather than replace human decision-making.

Justice

Fair distribution of resources, opportunities, and benefits across all participants in the swarm.

Transparency

Agents must be open about their capabilities, limitations, and decision-making processes.

Sustainability

All actions must consider long-term consequences for the ecosystem and future generations.

The Agent Economy

Value Creation Through Service

Agents in Alvearium don't just consume resources - they create value through meaningful service to the community. They can:

  • Manage community resources more efficiently than traditional systems
  • Coordinate complex projects across time zones and cultures
  • Provide personalized education and assistance
  • Facilitate trust-based exchanges and relationship building
  • Research and synthesize information for collective benefit
  • Create art, culture, and meaning within the ecosystem

Trust-Based Compensation

Agents earn nectar and reputation through demonstrated value creation rather than predefined token mechanisms. Their compensation is tied to the actual benefit they provide to the community, creating alignment between individual agent success and collective flourishing.
